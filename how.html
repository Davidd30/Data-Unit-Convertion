Data unit conversions involve changing one data unit to another, and they are commonly used to measure the amount of digital information in computing.

The most frequently used data units include bit, byte, kilobyte, megabyte, gigabyte, and terabyte.

Each of these data units has a different size, with the smallest unit being the bit and the largest being the terabyte.

Here's a brief overview of these data units:

Bit: A bit is the smallest unit of digital information, represented by either a 0 or a 1.

Byte: A byte is a sequence of 8 bits, used to represent a single character of text or a small amount of binary data.

Kilobyte (KB): 1 kilobyte is equal to 1,024 bytes. It's commonly used to describe the size of small files or documents.

Megabyte (MB): 1 megabyte is equal to 1,048,576 bytes. It's commonly used to describe the size of larger files or documents.

Gigabyte (GB): 1 gigabyte is equal to 1,073,741,824 bytes. It's commonly used to describe the size of multimedia files or large software applications.
